EVERTON’S 10-POINT PREMIER LEAGUE PENALTY REDUCED ON APPEAL.

The Premier League has revealed that Everton's point deduction has been lowered, which will greatly increase their chances of escaping relegation. Earlier in the season, the Goodison Park team received a ten-point punishment for violating the Profitability and Sustainability Rules (PSRs) of the league.

Sean Dyche's team's fortunes took a hit, plunging them into a perilous position near the bottom of the Premier League. However, following an appeal against the penalty, its severity has been reduced.

As a result, Everton's position in the table improves, moving up two spots ahead of Nottingham Forest and Brentford, now standing at 25 points. They're five points clear of Luton, who currently occupy the final relegation place.

A statement from the Premier League announced, "An independent Appeal Board has determined that Everton FC's breach of the Premier League's Profitability and Sustainability Rules (PSRs) for the period ending Season 2021/22 will result in an immediate six-point deduction. This comes after the club appealed against the independent Commission's decision in November 2023, which had imposed a 10-point deduction for the breach of the PSRs."

"The appeal, heard over three days earlier this month by an Appeal Board consisting of Sir Gary Hickinbottom (Chair), Daniel Alexander KC, and Katherine Apps KC, saw Everton FC appeal the penalty imposed on nine grounds, all related to the severity of the sanction rather than the acknowledgment of the breach, which the club admitted."

"The Appeal Board upheld two of the nine grounds presented by Everton FC, substituting the original 10-point deduction with a six-point deduction. This revised penalty takes immediate effect, and the Premier League table will be updated today to reflect these changes."
