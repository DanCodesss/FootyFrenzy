‘ They stopped running and caring’ Former United Coach, Ole Gunnar Solskjaer highlights what happened with his United side at his final moments.

Some of Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's Manchester United players allegedly ceased "running and caring" during his final match as the team's manager.

The Norwegian was terminated by the Red Devils in November 2021 following a 4-1 defeat against Watford.

Harry Maguire, the former United striker, said certain players didn't put in as much effort as they could have during that match, in which he was sent off in the second half.

There were a lot of low points, he told the National. I am among those who remember the bad times more vividly than the good ones. The conclusion at Watford, well you can’t go lower than that. At halftime, I was quite sure, so my team talk revolved about making the most of the final 45 minutes spent together and giving it their best. A few of them had ceased to run, showing concern. However, we performed admirably in the second half.

When Van de Beek entered the game, Donny scored. We ought to have won by a larger margin. After that, we were sent gone. 2-1 up until the ninetieth minute. The fact that the score was 4-1 loss sounds awful. Though it wasn't a 4-1 game, it was unfortunate to lose to Watford."

Despite leading United to their joint-best Premier League position in ten years—second place—in 2021, Solskjaer's stint at the club eventually ended in failure.

Solskjaer has not managed in football since the Red Devils' 2021 Europa League final loss to Villarreal on penalties.

The 50-year-old former manager of Molde was said to be on the short-term roster of Bayern Munich, prior to the announcement by current manager Thomas Tuchel that he would be leaving later this season.
