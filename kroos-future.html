Toni Kroos's Contract is nearing it's end and Madrid has made their decision.

According to German media site Sport Bild, there is a greater chance that Kroos will stay for an additional year because Carlo Ancelotti's contract has already been extended until 2026. Given the declining significance of fellow midfielder Luka Modric at the team, the Italian manager strongly believes in Kroos' strategic ability in the middle of the pitch and is pushing for his extension.

The Madrid hierarchy has been persuaded by Kroos's recent performances that he is a valuable asset, and negotiations to extend his contract are reportedly scheduled to begin at the end of March, with both sides eager to come to an understanding.

This is despite the rise of younger players such as Eduardo Camavinga and Aurelien Tchouameni, who continue to be a vital presence in Real Madrid's midfield.

At the conclusion of the current season, Kroos is said to have informed his entourage that he plans to either retire from professional football or remain with the Spanish giants.

Madrid is prepared to provide an offer to him for a new one-year deal, comparable to the one in 2023, should he decide to try his luck for additional hardware after the summer.

It's thought that Kroos is considering making a comeback from his international retirement to support Julian Nagelsmann's Germany in the forthcoming European Championship, should he receive a call-up.

Even Nevertheless, Madrid will always play a big role in his life since his family—including his wife and kids—have found solace in Spain.
