Erik Ten Hag Warns Antony about Manchester United Struggles.

To regain his starting position at Manchester United, Antony has been cautioned by Erik ten Hag to put in a lot of effort. The Brazilian, who Ten Hag signed for £82 million in 2022 from Ajax, has not lived up to the expected cost thus far and is no longer a fixture in the starting lines. This season, he has not yet assisted or scored a goal in the Premier League.

With Rasmus Hojlund sidelined due to injury for the next few weeks, there is less competition for minutes in United's attack. However, manager Ten Hag has made it clear that Antony is not guaranteed playing time, delivering a critical assessment of the 24-year-old's recent form.

"He has to prove a point, that he will do," Ten Hag emphasized to the media. "And he has big potential. He hasn’t shown it lately, but I know what his talent is, and it’s about proving his talent, and it’s about him. And, yeah, he will probably get his opportunities, right?"

Ten Hag highlighted the competition for places in the team, with players like Amad Diallo, who is returning from injury, and Omari Forson impressing in training. This increased competition means Antony will need to demonstrate his abilities on the training pitch to earn playing time.

Additionally, Ten Hag suggested that Marcus Rashford may not necessarily be confined to a central forward role, indicating flexibility in United's attacking options.

With Rasmus Hojlund sidelined due to injury, Manchester United find themselves without a natural striker, as Anthony Martial is also unavailable. Martial, who has had a disappointing spell at the club, is expected to depart this summer, according to manager Ten Hag, who acknowledges the player's desire for more success.

"You can’t ignore the facts," Ten Hag stated. "I knew that Martial's last full game was in January 2021 as well. But you do everything to get him fit, to keep him fit."

"We've provided him with opportunities. For example, he performed well against Everton. However, his overall contributions to the team have been insufficient. We expected more from him, to be honest."

"Bringing in another striker would mean completely ruling him out at the beginning of the season, which I believe is not the right approach."

Antony is contracted to Old Trafford until 2027, but reports suggest his future at the club is uncertain due to his failure to impress those in charge.
