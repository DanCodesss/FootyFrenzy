Ronaldo's net worth: The highest-paid athlete in the world, with enormous earnings.

In addition to being one of the best football players of all time, Cristiano Ronaldo is also one of the most paid athletes in history.

The celebrity from Al Nassr is often regarded as the face of football, and he earns the money to support that claim with his salary and endorsement deals.

Ronaldo has one of the most illustrious careers of any footballer in history, having played for Manchester United, Real Madrid, and Juventus and winning 35 titles altogether.

In addition, he holds the record for the most goals scored in professional football history. Off the field, he is the most followed person on Instagram, with over 600 million followers.

Because of all of his achievements, he has amassed a staggering fortune, which talkSPORT.com has calculated.

It is believed that Ronaldo is worth around £500 million. As of early 2023, Forbes said that he was making an astounding £107 million.

He now holds the title of highest-paid athlete in the world, surpassing basketball great LeBron James, rival Lionel Messi, and Kylian Mbappe.

But since then, this figure would have skyrocketed since he became the highest-paid footballer ever when he joined Al Nassr and quadrupled his pay.

The five-time Ballon d'Or winner's deal, which is reported to be worth £177 million year, includes a £62 million pay for football as well as marketing bonuses. It expires in 2025.

Ronaldo makes an incredible £14.75 million a month, or £3.4 million a week. To put it even more into perspective, the Real Madrid legend is earning £485k a day, or £337 every minute.

The only other individual to have a lifelong contract with the athletic giants is NBA player LeBron James, but Ronaldo has a significant sponsorship agreement with them.

In addition, he has endorsement agreements with Castrol, Livescore, Clear, Binance, PokerStars, Armani, Tag Heuer, Kickoff, Herbalife, and Binance.

Bro even has his own companies and brand.

Ronaldo founded CR7, a store that offers undergarments, apparel, leisure, and home goods.

Additionally, he owns a number of hotels operating under the Pestana CR7 brand in Lisbon, New York, Madrid, and Marrakech.

In addition, he and his business partner Georgina Rodriguez are involved in a hair transplant company.

Ronaldo's substantial wealth allows him to enjoy a luxurious lifestyle, and he has built an impressive collection of real estate investments.

Alongside a £15 million apartment in New York, he possesses a property valued at £500 million in Madrid.

Ronaldo and Rodriguez began leasing out their Madrid residence earlier this year for £8,800 monthly.

Moreover, reports suggest he possesses a private estate in his hometown of Madeira Island, Portugal.

Initially, when he relocated, Ronaldo resided in a hotel suite in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, estimated at £270,000 per month. However, he has since transitioned to a residence of his own.

He also possesses an impressive collection of cars, which he frequently flaunts on social media.

Among his prized possessions is a rare £8.5m Bugatti Centodieci, one of just ten ever produced.

In addition to the Centodieci, his collection includes a Bugatti Chiron, Bugatti Veyron Grand Sport Vitesse, along with several models from Aston Martin, Ferrari, Lamborghini, and Rolls-Royce.
