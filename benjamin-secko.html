City, Liverpool, and Arsenal are expected to bid €50 million to acquire Benjamin Sesko, the top player for RB Leipzig, against Barcelona.

Premier League giants Manchester City, Liverpool and Arsenal along with Barcelona are reportedly ready to fight for €50 million-rated Benjamin Sesko.

The 20-year-old RB Leipzig striker currently holds a contract until June 2028, featuring an exit clause set at €50m (£43m/$54m), expected to become active in the upcoming summer transfer window, as reported by BILD. Although the price might seem high for a player of Sesko's age, his recent performances indicate he could be a worthwhile investment.

Despite a sluggish start to the season, Sesko has acclimated well to life in Germany and has begun to shine on the field. His last three Bundesliga appearances showcased his goal-scoring ability, contributing to Leipzig's victories over Union Berlin and earning a point against Augsburg. Additionally, his display against Real Madrid in the Champions League demonstrated his potential, repeatedly threatening Andriy Lunin's goal throughout the match, despite a disallowed goal early on due to VAR.

Scouts from Liverpool, Manchester City, and Barcelona foresee Sesko's potential to become one of Europe's most lethal strikers if he consistently delivers in both domestic and European competitions.

They recognize the possibility for his value to surpass £100m in the future, making the initial investment of £43m appear favorable.

Sesko may encounter strong competition for playing time at clubs like Liverpool and Manchester City, where established strikers Darwin Nunez and Erling Haaland already feature.

However, neither club may be willing to overlook such a promising talent. Meanwhile, Barcelona seeks a successor to Robert Lewandowski, with Sesko emerging as a fitting candidate for the role.
