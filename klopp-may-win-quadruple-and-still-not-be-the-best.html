Even if Liverpool wins the quadruple this season, Jurgen Klopp may not be the best manager the club has ever had.

Liverpool defeated Chelsea 1-0 in the League Cup final on Sunday, under the direction of Jurgen Klopp, keeping the team on track to win the treble this season. The outcome also prompted the customary barrage of exaggeration over Klopp's alleged record as one of the team's finest managers ever.

One of the primary issues with debates of this nature is that many participants believe football began with the launch of the Premier League in 1992.

Does winning one league title in nine years qualify someone to be labeled as 'great' compared to others from the same era?

Sir Alex Ferguson's impressive tally of 13 Premier League titles with Manchester United makes such assertions seem ridiculous.

Pep Guardiola (5), Jose Mourinho (3), and Arsene Wenger (3) also boast more Premier League titles than Klopp.

Currently, Klopp has won as many Premier League titles as Claudio Ranieri, yet Ranieri doesn't receive hyperbolic praise.

When considering overall trophies, Klopp's achievements align with those of Gerrard Houllier and Rafael Benitez.

Winning more trophies this season could alter perceptions. Claiming two out of three on offer would elevate Klopp's status.

Achieving the quadruple would place Klopp on par with legends like Sir Kenny Dalglish and Bill Shankly.

However, Klopp has yet to establish a dynasty at Liverpool, unlike Ferguson at United or Paisley at Liverpool in the 1970s and 1980s.

Even with a quadruple, Klopp wouldn't surpass the achievements of Liverpool's most successful managers, such as Bob Paisley.
