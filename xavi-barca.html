Before the team's season descends even farther into despair, the manager need to be given the opportunity to go.

Xavi got up, turned around, yelled at the referee, and slammed his seat in the dugout during the 45th minute of Barcelona's La Liga match against Granada on Sunday. His wrath was well-founded; Barca had let in another soft goal, which gave a relegation candidate the opportunity to dart down the right flank, pass the ball into the middle, and score past Marc-Andre ter Stegen, the current La Liga MVP.

The job has taken a toll on Xavi's mental health and the pressure of being the face of one of the world's biggest brands. Club president Joan Laporta is losing patience, and Xavi should leave now rather than waiting until summer.

Meanwhile, Barcelona finds itself at a standstill, although not in a state of crisis. Despite concerns about inconsistent performances and vulnerabilities on both offense and defense, they are expected to comfortably secure qualification for the Champions League next season. Despite trailing league leaders Madrid by 10 points, Xavi's side maintains a five-point lead over fifth-placed Athletic Club.

With the third-highest goal tally in La Liga, largely attributed to Robert Lewandowski, who has scored 10 league goals despite speculation about a potential decline, Barcelona's prospects seem promising. Additionally, 16-year-old Lamine Yamal has been instrumental in securing several recent victories, suggesting long-term potential for the team.

Although Laporta voices discontent over Barcelona's current standing, he acknowledges the club's underperformance. Despite their talent and potential to contend for league titles, criticism is directed towards Xavi's tactical rigidity and squad mismanagement. However, the club remains resilient and may regain form in the upcoming weeks.

Despite these efforts, Barcelona's crisis stems from a lack of confidence rather than a complete breakdown in performance. A managerial change may provide the necessary rejuvenation for the team, with options like Hansi Flick and Marcelo Gallardo being considered.

Although a managerial change poses risks, it could revitalize Barcelona's fortunes and elevate the team to greater heights. The allure of managing Messi and competing for league titles may have diminished, but promising options exist to lead the club forward.
